Why I do it

I have spent the majority of my career in the high tech sector as an engineer enabling the fabrication of semiconductor chips. Now I want to harness the power of those devices and unleash my passion to craft code, bringing ideas to life. Since my exposure to machine language programming of process control hardware for my senior thesis project as an undergraduate chemical engineering student, I have always looked for opportunities to integrate my passion for programming into every role I've held. As one example, while working for a large semiconductor equipment vendor, I saw the need to have a utility which could translate the recipe files stored on our process tools into a neatly formatted Excel workbook replicating nearly identically what was displayed on the tool's monitor. Not only did our customers respond enthusiastically to my application, but the business division I worked for adopted its use on a global scale for their technical support teams. That's what I call job satisfaction I never want to work another day in my life - and as long as I'm programming, I never will.
